  • Innovation to access nature

    Zoom is a Swedish lightweight electric all-terrain four wheeler. You drive a Zoom sitting down, using arms and hands only. It has continuous 4-wheel drive performance under all conditions. The vehicle is for anyone, and for people with disabilities, it opens up a world of new possibilities, especially access to nature. Show more

    Barriers and obstacles in nature that were prohibitive to those who cannot walk freely are easily overcome thanks to this recreational vehicle. The range is approximately 40 km (25 miles) per battery charge. Accessibility is a policy priority in Sweden, aiming to enable people with disabilities to exercise their rights as citizens, and to fulfil their obligations. The principal goal of Sweden’s disability policy has long been to ensure that people in this group have power and influence over their everyday lives. To achieve this goal, the focus has been shifted from social and welfare issues to democracy and human rights. Show less

Personal data and contact details (name, address, email address and telephone number, demographic information and – when clearly justified considering the purpose of the processing or the importance of secure identification – personal identity (ID) number)

Technical data about your unit or Internet connection (IP address, cookies, electronic images, videos and similar track and trace technologies on websites and in email communication and also your geographic location and login details)

Information about your user behaviour (for example, your search patterns on the website, your response time for pages and how you use our services). Our websites and our email communication may include an electronic image (‘web signal’ or ‘pixel tag’)

User-generated information (content that you voluntarily register or publish, for example in conjunction with competitions and newsletter applications)

Technical information about this website’s accessibility

This website is mainly compliant with the Swedish accessibility regulations and WCAG 2.1 level AA. Non-compliant content is listed below.

General issues

  • All HTML does not validate. We are currently working on fixing this for future updates.
  • Headlines are visually cut when using browser zoom on small screens, making them difficult to read and understand. We are currently working on fixing this for future updates.
  • Every page has the same page title. We are currently working on fixing this for future updates.
  • Some links are missing a unique description. We are currently working on fixing this for future updates.
  • The (human) language of the webpage is not defined. We are currently working on fixing this for future updates.
  • Some error messages are to generic. We are currently working on fixing this for future updates.

Issues with usage without vision (blindness)

  • The image bank thumbnails (the mini version of each image) has no description. We are currently working on fixing this for future updates.
  • In some scenarios, e.g. doing a search or trying to select multiple categories, there is no method to bypass repeating blocks. We are currently working on fixing this for future updates.

Issues with usage with visual impairment (including colour blindness)

  • Some hyperlinks only use color as a visual cue (making them difficult to distinguish from the rest of the text). We are currently working on fixing this for future updates.

Issues with usage with physical impairment

  • There are some issues operating our website from the keyboard - focus is sometimes not visible and the focus order is not always sequential. We are currently working on fixing this for future updates.
  • In some scenarios, e.g. doing a search or trying to select multiple categories, there is no method to bypass repeating blocks. We are currently working on fixing this for future updates.
